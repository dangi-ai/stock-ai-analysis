Business Overview
India's #1 Water Purifier Brand — 45-Year Legacy
Eureka Forbes is the market leader in residential water purification under the iconic Aquaguard brand, and a pioneer in vacuum cleaners under Forbes. The company operates a large direct sales force (~7,000+) and an expanding D2C model. PE firm Advent International acquired a ~72.56% controlling stake from the Shapoorji Pallonji Group in 2022 for ~₹4,400 Cr via its SPV Lunolux Limited. The company is now listed on NSE (re-listed 2022) and is focused on premiumisation, margin expansion, and building recurring annuity revenue through AMC (Annual Maintenance Contracts).
Two Clear Revenue Streams: Products + Services (AMC)
Products: Water purifiers, vacuum cleaners, air purifiers, robotic cleaners (fast-growing). Services: AMC contracts providing annuity-like recurring income — AMC bookings grew double-digits for the 3rd consecutive quarter in Q3 FY26. The service layer is Eureka Forbes' structural moat.
5-Year Vision: 2× Revenue, 3× EBITDA
Management targets ₹5,400–5,600 Cr revenue (from ₹2,436 Cr in FY25) and ₹800–850 Cr Adj. EBITDA (from ₹285 Cr in FY25) within 5 years. This implies ~18% revenue CAGR and ~24% EBITDA CAGR — aspirational but achievable if execution sustains.

Valuation Assessment — OVERVALUED
54× TTM P/E is Priced for Perfection
At 54× trailing earnings, Eureka Forbes is priced at a steep premium vs. the broader market (Nifty ~22×). This valuation is justifiable only if the company delivers consistent 20%+ PAT growth for 4–5 years. Any earnings disappointment, macro slowdown, or competitive disruption would re-rate the stock downward. Peers in consumer durables (Havells, Blue Star, V-Guard) trade at 40–60× — making it relative premium within the sector too.
EV/EBITDA of 30× — Sector High
At 30× FY25 EBITDA, this is significantly above traditional FMCG/consumer durable median of 20–25×. The market is ascribing a brand premium (Aquaguard moat) plus a growth re-rating. Justifiable only with sustained margin expansion toward 14–15%.
Forward View: P/E Compresses as Earnings Grow
If the company delivers management's guidance trajectory — revenue growing to ₹3,300 Cr by FY28, EBITDA to ~₹450–500 Cr, and PAT to ~₹280–300 Cr — forward P/E compresses to ~29–31× by FY28. That's still not cheap, but more reasonable. The 5-year return profile is therefore moderate, not spectacular, given the high entry price.
UBS Initiates Buy With 45% Upside Target
UBS initiated coverage with a Buy rating, citing market share gains, D2C expansion, and AMC monetisation. UBS expects Eureka Forbes to gain an additional 1.5% market share by FY28, backed by 30 new product launches. However, this bullish case is contingent on flawless execution.

Growth Drivers — What's Accelerating
1. AMC Bookings — Double-Digit Growth for 3 Consecutive Quarters
Annual Maintenance Contracts are the highest-margin, recurring revenue component. Growing double-digits means Eureka Forbes is successfully converting its installed base into an annuity-like business model. This is the structural moat.
2. Robotics & Air Purifiers — New High-Growth Verticals
Robotic vacuum cleaners (Forbes) and air purifiers (Aquaguard) drove Q3 FY26 outperformance. These categories are early-stage in India and growing fast. Both carry higher margins than traditional product categories.
3. D2C Expansion & Subscription Model
In Feb 2025, Eureka Forbes expanded D2C presence with subscription-based filter replacement and WiFi-connected smart purifiers. This reduces channel dependency and builds direct customer relationships — key for long-term margin improvement.
4. Premium Product Mix Shift (Margin Tailwind)
Premiumisation across water purifiers is driving ASP (average selling price) higher, which expands gross margins. Management's target to reach 14–15% EBITDA margins from current 11.9% is largely dependent on this mix shift succeeding.
5. International Presence (Small Base)
Eureka Forbes has international operations in select emerging markets. Contribution is small but adds optionality. No material revenue impact in 5-year view.
Growth Rating: ACCELERATING
Revenue CAGR: ~11–13% over FY24–26  |  PAT CAGR: ~30%+ over FY23–FY25
The combination of consistent double-digit revenue growth, expanding EBITDA margins (7.4% → 11.9%), and accelerating PAT (low base) paints a clear ACCELERATING trajectory. The key risk is whether margins can continue expanding to reach management's 3× EBITDA target.

✅ Virtually Debt-Free — D/E of 0.02×
With only ₹91 Cr in total debt against ₹310 Cr in cash, Eureka Forbes has a net cash position of ~₹219 Cr. There is no leverage risk. The company can fund growth initiatives, new product launches, and technology investment without needing debt financing or equity dilution.

The ₹5,162 Cr in goodwill/intangibles constitutes ~82% of total assets. This was created when Advent International acquired control in 2022 at a ~4.4× book premium. If business performance deteriorates, goodwill impairment could create sudden large losses on the P&L — a key tail risk for long-term investors.

Eureka Forbes outsources manufacturing and operates through a direct sales force + D2C channel. This asset-light model supports good free cash flow generation relative to capital employed, enabling reinvestment in brand, R&D, and channel without heavy capex.

Understanding the Low ROE — Structural Explanation
ROE is Suppressed by Acquisition Goodwill — Not Operational Weakness
Eureka Forbes' ROE appears low (2.3–3.7%) because the denominator (equity) is inflated by ~₹5,162 Cr of goodwill from Advent's ₹4,400 Cr acquisition in 2022. On a tangible equity basis (which is negative), the business generates strong operating cash flows. As PAT grows 15–25% annually, ROE will improve rapidly even without equity base changes. This is a known structural distortion, not a fundamental weakness in the business model.
Pre-Tax ROIC on Operating Assets is Healthy
The water purifier + AMC business is asset-light. On operating capital (working capital + fixed assets, ex-goodwill), the business earns healthy returns. The direct sales model (minimal inventory, large workforce but variable AMC income) creates strong operating leverage as revenue scales.

Key Assumptions & Risks to Projections
Key Upside Risk: AMC Penetration Exceeds Expectations
AMC/service revenue carries higher margins (~20–25%+ vs. 10–12% product margins). If AMC bookings continue double-digit growth and penetrate the ₹15,000 Cr non-AMC aftermarket opportunity (per management), revenue mix shift could drive margins above 14% faster than the base case.
Key Downside Risk: Market Share Erosion
KENT RO, A.O. Smith, Livpure, and new D2C players are aggressively expanding. If Aquaguard loses even 3–5% market share in water purifiers, revenue growth slows to 7–9%, and the bear case triggers. This is the highest-probability downside scenario.
PE Exit Risk: Advent International's Investment Horizon
Advent International acquired in 2022. Typical PE exit in 5–7 years implies a potential exit by 2027–29. A block deal of ~63% promoter holding hitting the market could suppress the stock price significantly during the exit window, regardless of fundamentals.

Eureka Forbes is a high-quality consumer brand (Aquaguard) with a structural moat in water purification — a category that benefits from India's secular water quality concerns, rising health consciousness, and premiumisation. The AMC (service contract) model creates recurring annuity income, and new categories (robotics, air purifiers) are growing fast. The business fundamentals are improving clearly: EBITDA margins have expanded from 7.4% to 11.9% in three years, and PAT has grown at 30%+ CAGR from FY23 to FY25.

However, the current valuation leaves little margin of safety. At 54× TTM P/E and ~30× EV/EBITDA, the market has already priced in strong execution. The stock is also 33% below its 52-week high of ₹668, suggesting the market has already partially re-rated it.

For a 5-year investor: Eureka Forbes is a HOLD/ACCUMULATE at current levels. The risk-reward improves significantly if the stock corrects toward ₹350–380 (bear case re-rating scenario), where the forward P/E compresses to ~37–40×. At current ₹447, the risk-reward is balanced but not compelling — suited for investors who believe in the management's 2× revenue / 3× EBITDA target. The PE overhang (Advent International's likely exit by 2027–29) is a meaningful timing risk.

Competitive Position in Water Purifiers
Eureka Forbes Overtook KENT in RO Water Purifiers (TechSci Research)
Per TechSci Research, Eureka Forbes reclaimed the #1 position in India's RO water purifier segment from KENT RO. The Aquaguard brand benefits from 45 years of customer trust, a large installed base, and a network of trained service engineers — advantages that are difficult for new entrants to replicate quickly.
Competition is Intensifying — 10+ Brands Now Active
The India water purifier market includes: Eureka Forbes (Aquaguard), KENT RO, Livpure, A.O. Smith, Blue Star, Havells (Havells Delite), Panasonic, LG, HUL (Pureit), and multiple D2C brands. Entry-level RO prices have fallen significantly, creating margin pressure in the mass segment. Eureka Forbes is responding with premiumisation and AMC-focus.
Global Water Purifier Market Context
Global water purifier market valued at USD 35.6B in 2025, growing at 8.3% CAGR through 2032. India is among the fastest-growing markets. Eureka Forbes is a leading participant globally, giving it R&D depth and potential for international expansion beyond India.
Relative Valuation Commentary
Eureka Forbes Trades at Peer-Comparable Multiples, But with Inferior ROE
Eureka Forbes' 54× P/E is comparable to Havells (58×) and Blue Star (55×). However, both Havells and Blue Star have ROEs of 18–22%, versus Eureka Forbes' 3.7%. This means Eureka Forbes is paying a consumer brand premium without the capital efficiency to justify it currently. As PAT grows and the equity base stabilises, ROE should improve — but investors should demand this before ascribing a full-peer premium valuation.
