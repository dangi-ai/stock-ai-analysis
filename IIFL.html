⚠ RBI REGULATORY ACTION HISTORY
RBI banned IIFL Finance's gold loan disbursements for 6.5 months in FY25, citing compliance deficiencies in gold assaying and LTV practices. Ban lifted Sep 2024. This shows regulatory risk is real — any repeat action could be severely disruptive to operations.
⚠ LOW PROMOTER HOLDING (~25%)
Promoter stake has declined to ~24.9%, which is low for an NBFC. Low promoter skin-in-the-game can be a governance watch point. The stock has ~38% held by public/retail, creating sensitivity to sentiment shifts.
⚠ INCOME TAX SPECIAL AUDIT (Jan 2026)
Income Tax Department ordered a special audit for IIFL Finance for a specific block period. Company states it is "procedural in nature." However, this creates near-term uncertainty around potential tax liability. Outcome not yet known.

Definitions — tap to expand

P/E ratio — What you pay per ₹1 of profit. Lower vs history and sector = cheaper.

P/B ratio — Price vs what the company actually owns (book value). For NBFCs, below 2x is generally considered cheap.

EV/EBITDA — Full business value check including debt. Lower = better value.

ROE — For every ₹100 shareholders invested, how much profit did the company make. Above 15% = good for NBFC.

ROCE — How efficiently the whole business uses capital including borrowed money. Above 15% = healthy.

CRAR — Capital to Risk-weighted Assets Ratio. RBI minimum for NBFCs is 15%. IIFL at 27.7% is well-capitalised.

GNPA / NNPA — Gross/Net Non-Performing Assets. Lower is better. Below 2% is healthy for a diversified NBFC.

NIM — Net Interest Margin. The spread between what an NBFC earns on loans vs what it pays on borrowings. Above 10% is healthy for IIFL's business mix.

AUM — Assets Under Management. Total loan book size. Growing AUM = growing revenue base.

Free Cash Flow — Cash left after all expenses and investments. Positive and growing = genuinely healthy business.

CAGR — Compound Annual Growth Rate. The average yearly growth rate over a period.
