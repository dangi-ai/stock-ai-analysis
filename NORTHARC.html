⚠ TRADING BELOW IPO PRICE
At ₹207, the stock is ~21% below its IPO price of ₹263 (Sep 2024). This reflects: (1) MFI sector stress impacting sentiment for all microfinance-adjacent NBFCs; (2) post-IPO P/E derating; (3) FY25 Q4 PAT drop of 55%. However, Q3 FY26 delivered record quarterly profits — business fundamentals are recovering.
⚠ NO TRADITIONAL PROMOTER (0% PROMOTER HOLDING)
Northern Arc is a PE/institutional investor-backed company with 0% promoter holding. Top shareholder is Leapfrog Financial Inclusion India II Ltd at 16.18%. This is structurally different from a founder-led company — governance accountability, succession planning, and management incentive alignment work differently. Not inherently bad, but warrants monitoring of key management continuity.
ℹ UNIQUE BUSINESS MODEL — PLATFORM + LENDER
Unlike most NBFCs that only lend, Northern Arc also runs a debt placement/co-lending platform (Nimbus, nPOS). This generates fee-based income independent of its own balance sheet, offering earnings diversification. D2C direct lending AUM is ₹8,492 Cr and credit funds AUM is ₹3,207 Cr out of total ₹15,121 Cr AUM.

Why is it trading at a deep discount?
1. MFI sector headwinds: Industry-wide NPA stress in microfinance (FY25 H2) dampened sentiment for all MFI-adjacent lenders. Northern Arc has ~15-20% AUM in microfinance through its originator network.

2. ROE below 15%: At 9-10% ROE, the stock arguably deserves to trade below book. However, this reflects IPO equity dilution — the capital needs time to get deployed and generate returns.

3. Post-IPO correction: Listed at ₹350+ (33% premium to ₹263 IPO price) and has since corrected ~40% from peak. Much of the froth has been washed out.

The case for rerating: As ROE recovers toward 13-15%, P/B expansion toward 1.5-2x is historically normal for quality NBFCs. At P/B 0.91x, the risk-reward is skewed positively if fundamentals deliver.
Overall valuation: UNDERVALUED
At P/B below 1x and P/E at a 47% discount to peer median, NORTHARC is one of the cheapest mid-size NBFCs on the board. The discount reflects near-term concerns (MFI stress, ROE recovery), not a broken business. Record Q3 FY26 profits suggest the worst may be behind it.

Definitions — tap to expand

P/E ratio — What you pay per ₹1 of profit. 11x means you pay ₹11 per ₹1 of annual earnings.

P/B ratio — Price vs book value. Below 1x means you're buying ₹1 of net assets for less than ₹1 — technically buying at a discount to what the company owns.

ROE — For every ₹100 of shareholder equity, how much profit. Above 15% = good; below 10% = concerning.

CRAR — Capital to Risk-weighted Assets Ratio. RBI minimum for NBFCs is 15%. NORTHARC at 24.7% has ample headroom to grow the loan book.

GNPA / NNPA — Gross/Net Non-Performing Assets. 0.45% GNPA for NORTHARC is exceptional — peers typically run 1.5-2.5%.

NIM — Net Interest Margin. Spread between what Northern Arc earns on loans (~15-16%) and pays on borrowings. Higher NIM = more profitable lending.

D2C — Direct-to-customer lending from Northern Arc's own balance sheet (vs marketplace where it places debt from third-party capital).

AUM — Assets Under Management. Total loan book. Growing AUM = growing revenue base.

PAT/AMA — Profit After Tax as % of Average Managed Assets. The NBFC equivalent of return on assets. 2.2-2.3% is moderate.

CAGR — Compound Annual Growth Rate. The average yearly growth rate over a period.
