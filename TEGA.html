Business Overview — What Does Tega Do?
Global Niche Leader — Mining Wear & Consumables Since 1976
Tega Industries designs, manufactures, and services wear-resistant consumables for mineral beneficiation and material handling. Its core products are mill liners (rubber, composite, steel, polyurethane) that line the inside of grinding mills, protecting them from abrasion while optimising throughput. These liners are mission-critical, regularly replaced consumables — roughly 70–80% of mill liner demand is replacement, not new installations. Tega serves copper, gold, iron ore, lithium, nickel, and phosphate miners across 92+ countries with >80% of revenue from exports.
Annuity-Like Revenue from Replacement Cycle
Mill liners wear out over 6–18 months and must be replaced to prevent catastrophic failure. Once Tega is embedded with a mining client, switching costs are high (recertification, site-specific engineering, installed field service teams). This creates sticky, recurring, high-margin revenue with multi-year supply agreements — similar to a consumables model.
The Molycop Bet — From ₹1,700 Cr to $2B+ Revenue Company
Molycop is the world's largest grinding media (steel balls) manufacturer, supplying the complementary consumable in the same mill system where Tega's liners sit. Combining Tega's polymer liners with Molycop's grinding balls creates a full mill optimization offering — the only global provider of both product lines. Molycop's revenue is ~$800M–1B USD annually. If the integration succeeds, Tega-Molycop becomes a ~$1B+ USD revenue industrial giant by FY27–28.

Risk Flags
🔴 Molycop Integration Risk — $1.48B Deal on ₹11,912 Cr MCap Company
The Molycop acquisition is ~100% of Tega's current market cap. Funding requires ₹1,500 Cr in new debt + ₹3,517 Cr OCRPS investment in the acquisition HoldCo. This will materially increase consolidated leverage. Integration of a $800M+ USD global business by an ₹1,700 Cr Indian company is operationally complex and carries meaningful execution risk.
⚠️ Q3 FY26 PAT Collapse — 63.7% YoY Decline
While most of Q3 FY26's PAT decline is attributed to one-time acquisition costs and Labour Code provisions (₹63 Cr consolidated), the EBITDA margin also contracted from 24% to 14% — a 1,000 bps drop. Operating expenses (employee + other) surged without corresponding revenue growth. Management says adjusted margins are above 20%, but the reported numbers concern investors.
⚠️ High Valuation — 59.5× TTM P/E on Compressed Earnings
TTM P/E of 59.5× is elevated even adjusting for the one-time charges. The stock is valued on the Molycop optionality, not current earnings. If the deal synergies take longer to materialise, the stock may underperform vs its premium valuation.
⚠️ Promoter Holding Declined 7.53% Last Quarter
Promoter stake fell from ~75% to 67.3% in the December 2025 quarter — a significant drop, possibly related to the Molycop acquisition financing structure (OCRPS/equity issuance). Further dilution is possible as the deal closes.
✅ Pre-Acquisition Balance Sheet Was Strong
Before Molycop funding, Tega had D/E of just 0.08×, interest coverage of 27.6×, and current ratio of 2.4×. The underlying operating business is financially robust; the risk is purely from the acquisition leverage.

Valuation Assessment — OVERVALUED at Current Price
59.5× P/E on Deteriorating Earnings — Market Pricing in Molycop Optionality
At ₹1,663, the market is NOT valuing Tega on its current earnings (which are declining in FY26). It is pricing in the post-Molycop combined entity scenario. The current 59.5× P/E on compressed TTM earnings makes the stock appear expensive on a standalone basis. However, if Molycop consolidation is successful and Tega reports EPS of ₹50–70 by FY28, the current price may look reasonable in hindsight. This is high-conviction or nothing — there is limited middle ground.
P/B of 8.53× — Justified for Niche Global Leader Pre-Deal
Before the Molycop announcement, Tega typically traded at 6–9× P/B based on its niche market leadership, high gross margins (~60%), global customer relationships, and recurring revenue. The P/B premium reflects intangible value (customer relationships, technical know-how, global service network) not captured in book value.
Post-Molycop: Valuation Needs Re-Base
Once Molycop is consolidated, Tega's consolidated revenue will jump from ~₹1,700 Cr to ~₹8,500–9,000 Cr ($800M+ Molycop + $200M Tega). Debt will rise significantly (~₹4,000–5,000 Cr gross). EV/EBITDA on the combined entity at current MCap + new debt is a more meaningful metric post-FY27. The acquisition is EV-dilutive in the short run but potentially value-accretive if margins are maintained.

Near-Term Growth Pain — Q3 FY26 Margin Compression Explained
EBITDA Margin: 24% → 14% in One Quarter — Why?
1. One-Time Acquisition Costs: Professional fees, advisory costs, and transaction expenses related to the Molycop deal are hitting the P&L in FY26. These are non-recurring.

2. Labour Code Provisions: Indian Labour Code implementation (effective Nov 21, 2025) required recognition of past service costs — ₹63.23 Cr (consolidated) charged to Q3. Entirely one-time.

3. Employee Cost Surge: Employee expenses grew 15% YoY vs revenue decline of 0.7% — partly structural expansion ahead of Molycop integration.

Management's clarification: Adjusted for the Labour Code charge and acquisition costs, underlying EBITDA margins are >20% — consistent with the FY25 level. Gross margin improved to 60% from 59% YoY — the core operating model is intact.
Organic Growth Drivers Remain Strong
• Global copper, gold, and lithium capex cycles are at decade-highs — driving demand for mill liners in Latin America, Africa, and Australia
• >70–80% of mill liner market is replacement-driven — highly predictable
• Order book of ₹1,140 Cr (Dec 2025) with ₹810 Cr executable within 1 year
• Market share gains in polymer mill liners vs. steel alternatives (polymer is more efficient, lower maintenance)
• New product launches in ceramics and composite liners for harder ore types
Post-Molycop: Revenue Could Jump 5–6× by FY27–28
Molycop's standalone revenue is estimated at $800M–1B USD (~₹6,700–8,400 Cr). Adding Tega's organic ₹1,700–1,900 Cr, the consolidated revenue could reach ₹8,500–10,000 Cr by FY28 at 77% consolidation. This would make Tega-Molycop one of India's largest mid-cap industrial exporters by revenue.
Global Mining Cycle Context — Tailwind
Copper, Gold, Lithium Supercycle = Sustained Mill Liner Demand
The global energy transition (EVs, solar, wind) requires massive copper and lithium mining. Gold mining continues driven by institutional demand and de-dollarisation. Both sectors are in multi-year capex expansion cycles. Mill liners are a direct play on ore processed, not commodity prices — making Tega a relatively defensive way to play the mining supercycle.
Mill Liner Market Growing at 5.4% CAGR to $6.65B by 2033
Global mill liner market projected at $4.31B in 2025, reaching $6.65B by 2033 (CAGR 5.36%). Tega's organic growth has consistently outpaced this at 8–15% due to market share gains. Post-Molycop, the combined entity would represent ~$1B+ of this market.

Post-Molycop: Balance Sheet Transformation
🔴 Leverage Will Jump Significantly Post-Acquisition
Tega will draw ₹1,500 Cr in borrowings and subscribe to ₹3,517 Cr in OCRPS of the acquisition HoldCo (Tega HoldCo). This investment is structured off-balance-sheet at the parent level initially, but will flow through on consolidation. Total deal-related outflow is ~₹5,000+ Cr. D/E could move from 0.08× to potentially 0.5–0.8× consolidated, depending on Molycop's own debt level (est. $300–400M USD). Covenants and refinancing risk become real post-close.
⚠️ OCRPS Structure Adds Complexity
The use of Optionally Convertible Redeemable Preference Shares (OCRPS) in the HoldCo structure introduces complexity. Depending on conversion/redemption terms, these could create future dilution or cash drain. OCRPS terms are not fully public. This is an uncertainty for long-term modelling.
✅ Molycop Has Strong Cash Flows to Service Acquisition Debt
Molycop, as the world's largest grinding media manufacturer (~$800M+ revenue), generates substantial EBITDA (~$100–150M USD est.). The combined entity's EBITDA should comfortably service the $1.48B acquisition debt over 5–7 years, assuming no major commodity cycle downturn. Management has guided for deal accretion from FY27.

Why Tega's Returns Are Genuinely Good
60% Gross Margin in a Capital-Intensive Sector is Exceptional
Most industrial manufacturers operate at 25–40% gross margins. Tega's ~60% gross margin reflects: (a) the proprietary rubber/polymer formulations it has developed over decades; (b) the high switching costs embedded in field service contracts; (c) the mission-critical, non-commodity nature of its products. This gross margin moat is hard to replicate and is the core value driver.
Interest Coverage of 27.6× — Best-in-Class for an Industrial
With debt-free balance sheet in FY25, every rupee of operating profit flows to equity holders. This is why PAT margins of ~12% are achievable alongside 23% EBITDA margins — minimal interest cost leakage.
ROE Declining: 16.3% → 13.4% — The Molycop Effect
ROE decline from FY24 to FY25 is partly due to increasing equity base (retained earnings accumulation) and slightly lower PAT. Post-Molycop, new debt will change the capital structure significantly, and ROE trajectory will depend on Molycop's ROCE contribution. If accretive, ROE can improve. If integration struggles, ROE falls further.

⚖️ MODERATE — High-Quality Business, High-Stakes Bet
5-Year Investment Horizon  ·  Analysis Date: 31 Mar 2026
Tega Industries is one of India's most genuinely unique industrial businesses — a niche global leader in mining wear solutions with 60% gross margins, recurring replacement revenue, and presence in 92 countries. The business quality is unambiguously high: sticky customer relationships, technical moat in polymer formulations, and direct exposure to the secular copper/gold/lithium mining supercycle. Pre-Molycop, Tega was a high-quality compounder trading at a premium but justifiable valuation.

But the Molycop acquisition changes everything — for better or worse. At $1.48 billion EV (~100% of Tega's current market cap), acquiring the world's largest grinding media manufacturer is either Tega's defining moment or its biggest risk. The strategic logic is compelling: mill liners + grinding media = complete mill optimization, the only global provider of both. But the deal requires ₹1,500 Cr in new debt and ₹3,517 Cr in OCRPS, and integrating a multi-billion-dollar global business with operations across Latin America, North America, and Australia is operationally daunting for a ₹1,700 Cr revenue Indian company.

The near-term picture is messy: Q3 FY26 PAT collapsed 63.7% due to acquisition costs and Labour Code provisions. EPS is declining. The stock is down 21.7% from its 52-week high. At 59.5× TTM P/E on compressed earnings, the valuation is high and the margin of safety is thin.

For a 5-year investor: The investment thesis pivots entirely on Molycop integration. If it succeeds — revenue 5× by FY28, EBITDA doubles, synergies flow — this is a multi-bagger. If integration struggles or mining capex cycles turn down, the leveraged balance sheet and rich valuation create a painful bear case. This is a MODERATE — not because the business is average, but because the risk-reward at current valuations is balanced, not one-sided. Best suited for investors who have conviction in the Molycop thesis and can tolerate 2–3 years of earnings noise.

Global Market Context
Tega Holds ~10–12% Global Market Share in Polymer Mill Liners
In the ~$4.3B global mill liner market, Tega is the #2 polymer mill liner manufacturer by revenue, behind Metso. Global market share ~10–12% in its specific segment. Key competitive differentiation: Tega's liners are lighter (polymer vs. steel), last longer in softer ore conditions, and reduce energy consumption. The polymer shift from steel liners is a multi-year structural tailwind.
Post-Molycop: Tega Becomes #1 or #2 in Global Grinding Media
Molycop is the world's largest grinding media (forged steel balls) manufacturer with operations in Latin America, North America, and Australia. Post-acquisition, Tega-Molycop becomes the world's only integrated mill liner + grinding media company — a unique moat that neither Metso, Weir, nor FLSmidth currently possesses.
India Premium vs Global Peers Is Extreme at Current Levels
Metso and Weir trade at 22–25× P/E despite having much larger scale, lower execution risk, and similar (or better) EBITDA margins. Tega at 59.5× P/E is pricing in Molycop synergies, India growth premium, and promoter execution quality — all forward-looking assumptions that carry significant uncertainty.
